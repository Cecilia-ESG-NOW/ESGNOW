Derivatives

ASAGAO Capital · Forwards · Futures · Options · Hedging · Speculation · Arbitrage

This section introduces the structure, valuation, and economic functions of derivative instruments. The lectures examine how forward contracts, futures contracts, and options are used to transfer risk, manage market exposure, discover prices, and support financial decision-making.

The objective is to develop a systematic understanding of derivative markets, contract specifications, payoff structures, no-arbitrage relationships, margin systems, clearing mechanisms, and the roles of hedgers, speculators, and arbitrageurs.

Learning Focus

  • Understand the economic purpose and basic structure of derivative instruments.
  • Distinguish among forward contracts, futures contracts, and options contracts.
  • Identify the rights, obligations, and payoff structures of long and short positions.
  • Explain how hedgers use derivatives to reduce or manage financial risk.
  • Explain how speculators assume market risk in expectation of earning a return.
  • Understand how arbitrage supports no-arbitrage pricing and market efficiency.
  • Examine the standardized specifications of exchange-traded futures contracts.
  • Understand margin accounts, daily settlement, marking to market, and clearinghouses.
  • Build a foundation for futures pricing, option valuation, and derivative risk management.

Derivative Markets Principle

Derivatives do not eliminate financial risk. They transfer, redistribute, transform, and price risk through contractual relationships.

A disciplined derivatives user does not simply ask, “Will the market rise or fall?”

A disciplined derivatives user asks: “What exposure does this contract create, what risk does it transfer, what obligations arise at maturity, and how does the position affect the overall risk of the portfolio?”
