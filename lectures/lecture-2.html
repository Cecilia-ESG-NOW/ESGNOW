ESG Bank Lending & Corporate ESG Profiles and Banking Relationships

Lecture 1 · Finance & ESG · Bank lending · EN / CN

Lecture overview: ESG information, lending incentives, and bank–firm relationships.

This lecture examines how corporate ESG profiles influence bank lending decisions and the formation of long-term banking relationships.

We explore ESG not merely as a disclosure metric, but as an informational signal that interacts with traditional credit risk assessment, monitoring incentives, and relationship lending dynamics.

Particular attention is paid to how banks incorporate ESG information into loan pricing, covenant design, and monitoring intensity, as well as how firms strategically manage ESG profiles to influence financing conditions.

The lecture situates ESG bank lending within the broader framework of asymmetric information, delegated monitoring, and the evolving role of banks in sustainable finance.
